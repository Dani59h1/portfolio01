BASIC UX

Berserk Rebellion, the clothes brand born from the theme Basic UX started out the same way as the previous theme. I put research into sustainable and eco friendly clothing, but also developed a survey to see how the targeted audience would react. I also interviewed a person from the targeted audience to get more in-depth answers, something I couldn’t do with a survey, however the survey gave me quantity over quality instead.

After processing the data from the research, survey and interview, I put together another moodboard and then a styletile for the upcoming design.

Sketching the idea down on a piece of paper for an ‘art museum’ I saw what worked well and focused my design on those aspects. I was open to, but only found minimal inspiration in others’ designs as the elements were either already included in mine or simply didn’t fit in.

I immediately saw the sense in creating a prototype. If I were to code this site I’d be relieved to have the prototype by my side. It let me make adjustments and move things around much faster than coding ever could.

The most difficult task would prove itself to be deciding what the brand stands for and why you as a consumer should care. Coming up with a great USP and Tag Line was no easy task, nor was the copywriting, microcopy etc. which would make the brand interesting and intriguing, as well as give a user-friendly experience.

The site was then tested with a ‘think-aloud test’ with three people and minor adjustments were made to optimize the page.
