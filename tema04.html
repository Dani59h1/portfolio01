ANIMATION

Yippee Ki Yay Cowboy. For this theme, a paper prototype was created at first to demonstrate how everything was going to work, then everything was sketched out in hand before starting to design it in Adobe Illustrator.

To show the flow of control from activity to activity, I created an Activity Diagram first. It gave me a great overview of the task at hand, but when it came to coding JavaScript, I first created a State Machine Diagram to emphasize the flow of control from state to state.

My experience with Illustrator wasn’t just limited, it was nonexistent. However the workflow wasn’t much different than many of the other Adobe programs, so designing was fun and smooth. Like any marriage we had our struggles, but we worked through it and came up with a flat-design looking Wild West game inspired by the Lucky Luke comics.

JavaScript was used to make the game playable. Any normal human would’ve given up long ago, but I forced through it on my own like Tom Hanks’ character in Cast Away. It can then be said I was upgraded to JavaScript guru and basically promoted to mage of the old town with the scrolls of these ancient languages I suddenly had in store. And the amount of townsfolk who needed their scrolls proofread and fixed was a line so long it stretched out beyond kingdom walls.

Much can be said about JavaScript, but in the end we parted as friends.
